The Story So Far

My journey started not with a campaign brief, but with a terminal window. Computer Science taught me how systems think — how data flows, how logic scales, how architecture holds under pressure.

But somewhere between building APIs and debugging authentication flows, I noticed something that textbooks don't teach: the most elegant code means nothing if nobody clicks the button. Users don't read documentation. They feel friction. They bounce. They forget.

That gap — between what engineers build and what users actually do — became my obsession. I started studying the other side: why certain landing pages convert and others don't, how an email subject line changes open rates, what makes someone scroll versus leave.

I don't just ask "does this work?" — I ask "does this make someone stay?"

I'm currently looking to bring that thinking into a real marketing team — where I can learn fast, contribute early, and grow at the intersection of technology and human behavior.
