The Story So Far

My journey didn't start with a canvas, but with a terminal window. Computer Science taught me the architecture of systems — how data flows, how logic scales, and how to build environments that hold steady under pressure.

But as I built more, I started feeling a gap. I saw technically "perfect" builds that were logically sound but visually cold — tools that functioned but didn't invite. That gap, between what works and what people actually want to use, is where design lives.

I don't see design as just aesthetic; it's the throughline that connects building and performance. A design only matters if it can be shipped (Development) and if it actually moves someone to act (Marketing). I live at that intersection: I design the experience, I build it myself to ensure every detail stays true, and I apply the psychology of what makes a user stay.

I don't just ask "does this build?" or "does this convert?" — I ask "does this feel like it was made by someone who cared?"

I'm looking to bring that design-first thinking into a team that builds things. Whether the title says design, development, or marketing, the mission is always the same: make something people actually want to use.
